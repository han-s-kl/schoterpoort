# STI (Sexually Transmitted Infection)

## What is an STI?

An STI is a sexually transmitted infection that can be asymptomatic. Symptoms may include yellow-green discharge, burning during urination, warts, blisters, sores, or yellow skin and yellow whites of the eyes. Early detection enables effective treatment.

More information can be found at [www.thuisarts.nl/soa](http://www.thuisarts.nl/soa).

## When to get tested?

- Most STI tests require waiting at least three weeks after exposure for reliable results
- HIV tests require three months for reliable results
- Testing can be done earlier if symptoms occur

## Testing methods per condition

- **Chlamydia/Gonorrhoea:** Urine or discharge samples
- **Syphilis/HIV/Hepatitis B:** Blood tests
- **Trichomonas:** Urine (men) or vaginal discharge (women)
- **Genital herpes/warts:** Clinical examination
- **Pubic lice:** Visual inspection

Tests are carried out at laboratory facilities; costs are subject to the excess (deductible).

## Rates 2025

- Basic rate: EUR 19.86
- Chlamydia + Gonorrhoea + Trichomonas: EUR 121.17
- Chlamydia + Gonorrhoea: EUR 80.78
- Chlamydia only: EUR 40.39
- Gonorrhoea only: EUR 40.39

View the complete [rate list Streeklaboratorium 2025](https://www.streeklabhaarlem.nl/wp-content/uploads/Tarievenlijst-2025.pdf).

## Free testing

The **[GGD Kennemerland](https://www.ggdkennemerland.nl/soa-test)** offers free STI screening for:

- Men who have sex with men
- People under the age of 25
- Sex workers

You can also visit the [Spaarne Gasthuis](https://spaarnegasthuis.nl/specialisme/interne-geneeskunde/soa-poli) for STI/HIV testing.
