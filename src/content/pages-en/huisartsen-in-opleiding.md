# GP trainees Amsterdam UMC (AMC)

Huisartsenpraktijk Schoterpoort is an accredited training practice of the University GP Institute and the basic medical training programme of Amsterdam UMC (AMC) in Amsterdam. The GP trainees have been qualified doctors for some time and usually have several years of experience working in hospitals and general practices.

GP trainees are trained in the practices of M. Louwet and W.H. Steketee; they are in the first year of their training. As of 1 March 2026, these are Jesse Holverda and Lazin Germawi. In the practices of doctor Louwet and doctor Steketee, junior medical students also complete a short two-week placement once a year.

## Jesse Holverda -- GP trainee with doctor Louwet

Since March 2026, I have been working as a GP trainee (HAIO) at Huisartsenpraktijk Schoterpoort under the supervision of GP M. Louwet and GP M. Moes. Before starting my GP training, I worked as a junior doctor at various healthcare institutions: I started at a GP cooperative in Amsterdam, then worked at the hospital in Aruba, and finally returned to the Netherlands to work in addiction care at Jellinek in Amsterdam.

I enjoy a varied working environment and believe I can find this in general practice. I practise my profession with patience and an attentive ear. And with a smile whenever possible. I look forward to meeting you during the consultation.

In my free time, I prefer to be outdoors as much as possible and together with family and friends. I exercise quite a lot (running, cycling), I love cooking and enjoy long dinners. I also like travelling as much as possible and enjoy visiting a museum or cinema.

## Donna Rog -- GP trainee with doctor Steketee

My name is Donna Rog and I am originally from Rotterdam. Since 2014 I have lived in Amsterdam, where I studied Medicine at the University of Amsterdam. After my studies, I focused primarily on Gynaecology and Obstetrics. I worked at OLVG and also spent 4 months in Tanzania, where I gained experience in obstetrics. After my time in gynaecology and obstetrics, I noticed that I missed the breadth of medicine. That is why I switched to the Emergency Department (ED), where I worked at OLVG. While working in the ED, I realised I was looking for a profession where I could support patients over the long term.

General practice offered exactly what I was looking for: the variety of daily care, the long-term contact with patients and the opportunity to support them at different stages of their care. I find it valuable to truly build a relationship with my patients and to help them with both physical and mental health issues.

I live with my partner in Amsterdam West, where I enjoy the vibrant city life. In my free time, I can often be found on the padel court or in a pilates class. I also love to travel and try to discover new places and cultures as often as possible.
