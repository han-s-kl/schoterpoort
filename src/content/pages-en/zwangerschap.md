# Pregnancy

## In brief

- Make an appointment with a midwife as soon as you are pregnant.
- You will have blood tests and an ultrasound.
- Eat healthily. Do not drink alcohol.
- If you smoke: ask your GP for help quitting.
- You can also get telephone help for quitting smoking.
- Take a folic acid tablet and a vitamin D tablet every day.

## Who helps you when you are pregnant?

### Calculating how far along you are

- Write down the date of the first day of your last period.
- Count the weeks from that date. That is how many weeks pregnant you are.
- You will give birth approximately 40 weeks after that date.

### Who helps you during your pregnancy and delivery?

Usually a midwife helps you during pregnancy and delivery. In some places in the Netherlands, a GP helps you. Make an appointment as soon as you know you are pregnant. Preferably before you are 9 weeks pregnant. After that, you will come for regular check-ups.

If necessary, you will receive help from a gynaecologist or midwife at the hospital. For example, if you are expecting [twins](https://www.thuisarts.nl/zwanger-van-tweeling) or if you [have diabetes and are pregnant](https://www.thuisarts.nl/diabetes-type-2/ik-heb-diabetes-en-ik-ben-zwanger). A gynaecologist is a doctor who specialises in pregnancy and childbirth.

### Who helps you if your pregnancy was unplanned?

Perhaps you have become pregnant and it was [not planned](https://www.thuisarts.nl/onbedoeld-zwanger/ik-ben-onbedoeld-zwanger-wat-kan-ik-doen). You may be unsure whether you want a child or not know at all.

Talk about it with someone you trust. Would you rather talk to someone else? Then make an appointment quickly with your GP, a midwife or an abortion clinic. You can talk about this in confidence. They can advise you on what you can do.

## Folic acid and vitamin D

### Folic acid

Take a tablet of 0.4 milligrams of folic acid every day. Continue until the end of week 10 of your pregnancy. Folic acid reduces the chance of a baby with spina bifida. You can buy folic acid at the chemist or pharmacy.

### Vitamin D

Take a tablet of 10 micrograms of [vitamin D](https://www.thuisarts.nl/vitamine-d/ik-heb-misschien-extra-vitamine-d-nodig) every day throughout your entire pregnancy. Vitamin D is needed for the growth of your baby's bones. Too little vitamin D may increase your risk of [gestational diabetes](https://www.thuisarts.nl/zwangerschapsdiabetes). And it may increase the chance that your baby is small at birth or wheezes. You can buy vitamin D at the chemist or pharmacy.

### No multivitamin

Do not take a multivitamin tablet. Multivitamin tablets usually also contain vitamin A. Too much vitamin A is harmful to your baby.

## Discuss with your doctor whether you may use medication

Are you taking medication, for example for an illness? Or do you want to start or stop a medication? Always discuss it with your doctor first. This also applies to medication, ointments, herbal remedies or drops that you bought yourself at a chemist or shop. Some products can be harmful to your baby.

Therefore, tell every doctor who treats you that you are pregnant. Also tell the pharmacist. Doctors and pharmacists know whether a product is safe or not when you are pregnant.

Paracetamol, for example, is allowed during pregnancy. Do you want to take paracetamol for more than a few days? Or a different painkiller? Then first ask your midwife or GP whether this is allowed.

## No alcohol, smoking or drugs

### No alcohol

Do not drink alcohol. Your baby's brain can be damaged. Your baby also grows less well. Do you still drink alcohol? Tell your midwife. And ask your GP for help quitting alcohol.

### Do not smoke and do not inhale others' smoke

Make sure that you and your partner do not smoke. If you smoke or inhale someone else's smoke, this is harmful to you and your baby. If you quit, you have a lower chance of problems during your pregnancy, delivery and after birth. Ask your GP for [help quitting smoking](https://www.thuisarts.nl/stoppen-met-roken/ik-ben-zwanger-en-wil-stoppen-met-roken).

You can also get free telephone help. Register at [Rookvrije Ouders](https://www.ikstopnu.nl/rookvrijeouders/).

### No drugs

Do not use drugs. Drugs can damage your baby's brain. Your baby also grows less well. If you do use drugs, discuss it with your midwife. Ask your GP for help quitting.

## Healthy eating, exercise and relaxation

### Eat healthily

Read about how to [eat and drink healthily when pregnant](https://www.thuisarts.nl/zwanger/ik-wil-gezond-eten-tijdens-mijn-zwangerschap).

### Exercise and sports

Make sure you [exercise for at least half an hour continuously every day](https://www.thuisarts.nl/gezonde-levensstijl/ik-wil-gezond-bewegen). You can continue doing most things as normal. Such as working, cycling, sports and fitness. As long as you feel comfortable doing so.

You may experience frequent Braxton Hicks contractions during exertion. This may be a sign that you need to take it easier.

Sports where you can easily fall or collide with others are best avoided. Scuba diving with oxygen tanks is also not allowed.

### Rest and relaxation

Also make sure to have moments of rest and [relaxation](https://www.thuisarts.nl/stress/ik-wil-me-beter-kunnen-ontspannen). Learn how to [manage stress](https://www.thuisarts.nl/stress/ik-wil-beter-omgaan-met-stress). Getting enough sleep is also important.

## Taking care of yourself

### Dental care

Brush your teeth well. This reduces the chance of developing an infection. Your baby could be born prematurely due to a gum infection.

### Wash your hands frequently

Wash your hands regularly with water and soap. Especially after contact with saliva or urine from young children.

### Wear gloves for the garden and litter box

Wear gloves when cleaning the litter box. Also do this when working in the garden, as cat faeces may be present. Cat faeces can contain a parasite (toxoplasmosis). If you contract it, this can be harmful to your baby.

## Sex during pregnancy

Sex during pregnancy is fine as long as you feel comfortable. It does not increase your risk of miscarriage or premature birth. You also do not have an increased risk of infection from bacteria or viruses.

[Does sex feel uncomfortable](https://www.thuisarts.nl/seksuele-klachten-vrouwen) or do you have less desire for sex? Discuss it with your partner. You can also discuss it with your midwife or GP.

### When should you not have sex?

In these situations, you should not have sex involving a penis, finger or dildo entering your vagina:

- Your membranes have ruptured. You notice this when a lot of fluid suddenly comes from your vagina (amniotic fluid).
- Blood is coming from your vagina.
- Your contractions start before you are 37 weeks pregnant.

## Working and travelling during pregnancy

### Working during pregnancy

Do you have heavy work or do you work at night? Or are there vibrations, radiation or chemical substances at your workplace? Then discuss with your employer or the occupational health physician whether your work can be adapted.

In some professions, you have a greater chance of contracting certain viruses or bacteria. For example, if you work in healthcare or childcare. You then have a greater chance of diseases such as chickenpox or fifth disease. These are diseases that can be harmful to your baby during pregnancy.

Discuss your work with your midwife. You can have blood tests done to see whether you have already had a disease.

### Travelling during pregnancy

Do you want to travel to a distant country? Ask your midwife or the GGD well in advance what you need to do. For example, to avoid certain diseases. Clearly state that you are pregnant.

Flying is not dangerous for your baby or you. However, after 32 weeks of pregnancy, the airline may refuse you boarding. Check this before booking a trip.

## Chemical substances, X-ray or scan during pregnancy

### No chemical substances

Make sure you do not touch or inhale chemical substances. Such as turpentine or insecticide. Do you want to paint or do DIY? Have someone else do it. And open the windows so that you do not inhale the fumes. Paint and DIY products contain substances that can be harmful to the baby.

Also preferably do not use nail polish remover with acetone.

### X-ray, CT scan or MRI

Are you having an examination involving an X-ray, CT scan or MRI? For example, at the hospital or dentist? Ask the doctor in advance whether it is safe and state that you are pregnant. Often the examination can proceed as normal.

## What check-ups and tests will you have during pregnancy?

### Check-ups by your midwife or gynaecologist

At each check-up, your midwife or gynaecologist discusses how you are doing. Your blood pressure is also measured. Your midwife or gynaecologist listens to your baby's heartbeat or checks whether your baby is growing well. Mention it if you have [complaints](https://www.thuisarts.nl/zwanger/ik-ben-zwanger-en-heb-klachten), for example [itching](https://www.thuisarts.nl/zwanger-en-jeuk/ik-ben-zwanger-en-heb-last-van-jeuk).

### First ultrasound

You will also have a first ultrasound. This shows exactly how far along you are in your pregnancy. It also shows whether your baby's heart is beating.

### Blood tests

It is important to have [blood tests](https://www.thuisarts.nl/zwanger/ik-ben-zwanger-en-krijg-bloedonderzoek) done at the beginning of your pregnancy. This will tell you whether you have substances in your blood that could make your baby ill. If you know this in time, you can be treated. This way your baby will not become ill.

### Testing for diseases and abnormalities in your baby

The midwife or gynaecologist will discuss whether you also want to have [other tests done](https://www.thuisarts.nl/onderzoek-baby-in-buik/ik-wil-beslissen-of-ik-baby-in-mijn-buik-laat-onderzoeken) on the baby. From 11 weeks you can have a blood test, for example for the risk of Down syndrome. At 13 weeks and 20 weeks you can have an ultrasound, for example for the risk of spina bifida.

### Whooping cough vaccination and flu jab

From week 22 of your pregnancy, you can get a [whooping cough vaccination](https://www.thuisarts.nl/kinkhoest/ik-ben-zwanger-en-wil-mijn-baby-beschermen-tegen-kinkhoest-22-wekenprik). From 22 weeks, you can also get a [flu jab](https://www.thuisarts.nl/griep/ik-denk-na-over-griepprik) in autumn. The midwife or gynaecologist will provide you with information about this.

### Childhood disease in someone you know

Call the GP if someone you know has a childhood disease that you yourself have never had. For example, [rubella](https://www.thuisarts.nl/rode-hond), [chickenpox](https://www.thuisarts.nl/waterpokken) or [fifth disease](https://www.thuisarts.nl/vijfde-ziekte). Or if someone you know has [whooping cough](https://www.thuisarts.nl/kinkhoest) and you are more than 34 weeks pregnant.

### Talking about delivery

Later in your pregnancy, you will talk with your midwife or gynaecologist about the delivery. You can then discuss where you want to give birth and [how you want to manage the pain](https://www.thuisarts.nl/omgaan-met-pijn-tijdens-bevalling/ik-denk-na-over-wat-ik-kan-doen-bij-pijn-tijdens-bevalling). And about what happens [after the delivery](https://www.thuisarts.nl/bevalling-en-erna).
